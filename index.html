What are the Ethels?

Ethel Haythornthwaite spent decades campaigning tirelessly for the right of ordinary people to walk the Peak District's moors and hills freely. At a time when vast swathes of upland were closed to the public, she lobbied, organised, and fought — and won. In 1951, the Peak District became England's very first National Park.

These 95 hills are named in her honour. Every summit you reach is a summit that Ethel helped to open up. So lace up your boots — you're walking in her footsteps.
